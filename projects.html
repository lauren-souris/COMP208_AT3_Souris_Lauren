Projects

Selected Food Technology & Industrial Arts work with outcomes, safety notes, and student impact.

Overview

This page highlights a selection of my university design and teaching projects. Each project demonstrates practical skill application, innovation, and alignment with student learning outcomes. The projects are designed to integrate inclusivity, sustainability, and creative thinking into practical classroom contexts.

Brock-n-Roll Bites — Endurance Nutrition Bar

A layered electrolyte bar tailored for endurance athletes, inspired by Ned Brockmann’s marathon journey. Focus: compact, nutrient-rich product delivering both fast and slow energy while replenishing electrolytes.

Students explored ingredient functionality, dehydration techniques, and recipe testing, with UDL options for adaptations, step visuals, and self-paced trials.

Key Focus: Nutritional science, product design, inclusive recipe development.

Puzzle Piece Chopping Board — Timber Fabrication

A woodworking project introducing measurement accuracy, tool safety, and craftsmanship. The puzzle-piece profile encouraged creativity while reinforcing practical joinery skills.

Students produced annotated sketches and step photos, building familiarity with SWMS and workshop expectations—linking design intent with safe execution.

Key Focus: Workshop safety, joinery precision, reflective design practice.

Automatic Plant Drip Feeder — Sustainable Systems

A sustainability-focused prototype introducing basic engineering through a self-watering feeder. Using recycled bottles, tubing, and adjustable nozzles, students built an effective irrigation system.

Through STEM integration, learners measured flow rates, modified nozzle designs, and tested efficiency— strengthening iterative design and environmental responsibility.

Key Focus: Sustainability, experimentation, problem-solving, eco-conscious innovation.
